Are we moving towards Zero-UI age ?

Zero UI is nothing but a concept of interacting to machines without any screen or interfaces. This enables us to communicate with machines in our simple colloquial language using behaviour and gestures. This is taking us to a new no-interface age. Complex machines are getting replaced by more complex ones but intelligent, empathetic, friendly and understanding at the same time. One example of such replacement is Amazon Echo replacing portable speakers in our homes. There are myriad examples like this.

If we extrapolate this situation of 'Zero UI' to have a glimpse of the future, we may end up replacing all electronic gadgets of our daily lives by some ultra-intelligent magical sensing chips. The chips may be embedded in human body which could do everything merely upon a gut sense or a neurone activity. Consequently, there would be no-machine houses with bare minimal living facilities/accessories sounding more like roti-kapda-aur-makan(food-cloth-&-house). This may even become 'roti-makan'(food-house), with kapda(cloth) replaced by digital bodypapers(on the lines of wallpapers), and bodaypapers could be changed in the blink of an eye.

 

The central idea is to get away with anything which comes in between the humans and the experience. One of such examples was the revolutionary invention of 'touch' instead of mouse clicks. Fingers side-cornered mouse and other such interfacing devices.

Now, on one hand we are heading towards making the human life independent of accessories and eliminating these hardwares which come in between the humans-as-it-is and the experiences, while on the other hand we are celebrating the return of digital pencils and pens. Yes, we are talking about Apple pencil in the world of zero UI, which seems pulling us back into the mechanical world.

Its quite appreciable that the purpose of Apple pencil is to enable us to do more intricate interactions with iOS devices, that could not have been possible otherwise. But we cannot deny with certainty that there could be no way to do intricate interactions without pencils, which could have been more humane and natural. This may sound as a sci-fi concept but those days are not too far. We have started living the days of Sci-fi stories conceptualised just decades ago. Doesn't this appear as a half-hearted attempt to solve the particular problem in the Zero UI era and that too from Apple. This also raises another question if its a saturation-stage of innovation at Apple now.

 

Consumers and connoisseurs of Apple devices always have a very high expectation in the field of technological innovation from Apple. There could perhaps been solved while staying on the same track of Zero UI, and making more humane and natural interactions possible.
